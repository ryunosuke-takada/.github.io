Core Research Themes

◾️解析的数論のソニフィケーション (Sonification of Analytic Number Theory)

リーマンゼータ関数の零点分布や素数分布といった数論的構造と、音響パラメータ（周波数、リズム、音色）との構造的対応を研究。ソニフィケーションによって、数理的概念と聴覚的情報を対応させ、数理構造の知覚に新たなアプローチを見出す。

◾️アルゴリズミック作曲と数理モデル (Algorithmic Composition & Mathematical Models)

クセナキス（Iannis Xenakis）の確率論的手法（Stochastic Music）、マルコフ連鎖、群論、非ユークリッド幾何学などの数理モデルを用いた作曲アルゴリズムを構築。ポリリズムとその知覚構造の対応。

◾️音響物理学と楽器設計への応用 (Acoustical Physics & Application to Instrument Design)

スネアドラム等の打楽器が発する音響の物理的特性（振動モード、減衰率、周波数特性）を、実験とシミュレーション（物理音響モデリング）によって定量的・数理的に評価。この知見を楽器設計に直接フィードバックし、音響特性を意図的にデザイン。

About Me

Born in 2007. Since childhood, I have harbored a strong interest in both mathematics and music (piano, percussion, composition/arrangement). Currently, focusing on the relationship between these two fields, I pursue interactive modes of expression that integrate them—such as composition using numerical algorithms and the introduction of auditory approaches to mathematics. I also apply my research findings to instrument design, handling everything from design and branding to performance consistently (Top prize at the Italy International Percussion Competition). Furthermore, based on my own experiences, I focus on Gifted and STEAM education in Japan, exploring diverse and creative educational models that are not bound by the frameworks of academia or art. My areas of interest span Physics, Topology, Instrument Design, AI, Programming, Oil Painting, Design, Haiku, and Rubik's Cube. Crossing the boundaries of academia, art, and craftsmanship, my central interest lies in creating new values and forms of expression through the harmony of mathematical thinking and sensibility.

Core Research Themes

◾️Sonification of Analytic Number Theory

Researching the structural correspondence between number-theoretic structures (such as the distribution of zeros of the Riemann Zeta function and prime number distribution) and acoustic parameters (frequency, rhythm, timbre). Through sonification, I map mathematical concepts to auditory information, finding new approaches to perceiving mathematical structures.

◾️Algorithmic Composition & Mathematical Models

Building composition algorithms using mathematical models such as Iannis Xenakis's Stochastic Music methods, Markov chains, Group theory, and Non-Euclidean geometry. Correspondence between polyrhythm and its perceptual structure.

◾️Acoustical Physics & Application to Instrument Design

Quantitatively and mathematically evaluating the physical characteristics (vibration modes, decay rates, frequency response) of sounds emitted by percussion instruments like snare drums through experiments and simulations (physical acoustic modeling). These findings are fed back directly into instrument design to intentionally design acoustic characteristics.
